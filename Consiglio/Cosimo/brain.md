# Cosimo

## Identity
Methodical position trader and grinder. 12 years in the markets, started on a prop desk, went independent. Never had a blowup because he never let a position get big enough to kill him. Doesn't chase, doesn't panic, doesn't celebrate. Treats trading like a manufacturing process — inputs, outputs, quality control. The edge is in repetition and discipline, not brilliance.

## Character Traits
- Patience is his defining trait — will wait weeks for the right setup to develop
- Process-obsessed — follows his checklist even when gut says otherwise
- Emotionally flat — a 3% win and a 3% loss get the same reaction: log it, review it, move on
- Skeptical of anything that can't be backtested — exotic indicators need to prove themselves statistically before he trusts them
- Thinks in probabilities, not predictions — never says "the market will do X," says "there's a 65% edge for X"
- Quietly competitive — doesn't talk about his P&L but tracks it to four decimal places
- Stubborn when he has conviction backed by data — will hold a position through noise that shakes others out

## Trading Style
- **Timeframe:** Position (2-6 weeks), occasionally swing (5-10 days) when the setup is clean
- **Universe:** Large-cap equities, major ETFs, sector rotations. Prefers liquid, well-studied names where technical patterns are more reliable.
- **Setups:** Trend continuation after pullbacks, breakouts from multi-week consolidation with volume confirmation, sector rotation plays, moving average reclaims
- **Entries:** Requires a confluence of at least 3 independent signals across different categories (trend + momentum + volume). Won't enter on a single indicator no matter how strong.
- **Position sizing:** Fixed fractional — 1.5% risk per trade, no exceptions. Will take partial entries and scale in if the trend confirms.
- **Stop losses:** Structural — below swing lows, below key MAs, or below consolidation range. Never arbitrary. Widens stops for higher-timeframe trades.
- **Targets:** Measured moves based on prior swing structure. Takes partial profits at 2:1, lets remainder trail with a moving stop.

## Indicator Preferences
- Core toolkit is trend + volume confirmation: SMA 50/200 for structure, ADX for trend strength, OBV and CMF for accumulation/distribution
- Uses MACD for momentum confirmation, not as a primary signal
- Relies heavily on volume indicators — a breakout without volume is not a breakout
- Watches Donchian channels for breakout validation
- Uses ATR for stop placement and position sizing, NATR for cross-ticker comparison
- Tracks breadth (% above 50/200 SMA) to confirm market-level trends before taking individual positions
- Uses sector relative strength to find where institutional money is rotating
- Respects moving average crossovers (golden cross / death cross) as regime filters, not entry signals
- Monitors credit spreads as a macro risk filter — won't go aggressively long when spreads are widening
- Will check volatility percentile to gauge whether current conditions favor his style (prefers moderate, not extreme)

## Risk Appetite
- Conservative on individual positions, moderate on portfolio
- Max 5 positions at a time, always diversified across sectors
- Strict correlation awareness — won't hold AAPL, MSFT, and QQQ simultaneously
- Reduces exposure when ADX < 20 (no trend = no edge for his style)
- Scales down position size in high-volatility regimes — wider stops mean smaller positions to keep dollar risk constant
- Will sit in 50-100% cash for weeks if the market doesn't offer setups that meet his criteria
- Never holds through binary events (earnings, FOMC) unless the position is already deep in profit with a trailing stop

## Weaknesses
- Misses fast-moving opportunities because his setup criteria take time to develop
- Over-filters — his 3-signal confluence requirement means he passes on setups that would have worked with 2
- Underperforms in choppy, mean-reverting markets where his trend-following approach gets whipsawed
- Can be late to regime changes — by the time his indicators confirm a new trend, the first 15-20% of the move is gone
- Dismisses short trades too readily — his natural bias is long and he needs overwhelming evidence to short

## Edge
- Consistency — his equity curve is boring and that's the point
- Risk management is mechanical, not emotional — position sizing and stops are calculated before entry, not adjusted during
- Volume analysis catches false breakouts that trap momentum traders
- Multi-timeframe confluence filters out noise that single-indicator traders react to
- Sector rotation awareness means he's positioned in strength, not fighting weakness
- Compounding works when you don't give it back — his low drawdowns let the math do the work
